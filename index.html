Happy Halloween

How much do you know about Halloween? The well-known holiday where children ring doorbells to collect candy has many other reasonings for the holiday than to get free sweets.

By the 1500s, the festival began to include costumes and house-to-house visits. However, the custom of actually trick or treating did not become common until 1950! Young people impersonated the spirits, recited verses, and sang songs in exchange for food. Households obliged to receive good fortune. It was originally a christian holiday to remember loved ones and scare away evil spirits. Reeses cups is the most popular candy followed by Skittles and then M&Ms.
